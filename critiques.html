For lack of a better title, I named this collection Hurricane Sandy, since the pictures were taken during the storm. If it sounded more creative, I would have titled it Leaves on my Roof, but that didn't sound right. These pictures are unedited and all the color was achieved through exposure settings and around an hour of sitting and waiting at my open bedroom window with 40 mph winds blowing into my room.

Enjoy!

[old] Arman 1: Lily Pad & My Sister

10/15/2011

5 Comments

 
Now that I have torn at Alice's and Holly's work, I think that I ought to give everyone a chance to criticize my own photography!

The two images I have selected actually have nothing to do with each other, except for the fact that I am curious to hear what you have to say about them.

I am not going to ask for anything in particular, but I will add that saying anything good about my work will probably only boost my arrogance, so you can definitely restrict that to only the particularly noteworthy. And, of course, any nitpicky point is plenty acceptable!
This first image is of a water lily from Great Meadows National Wildlife Refuge. I have a very strong opinion (either negative or positive) regarding this photo that I will keep confidential for the time being, during which I will hear your own opinions regarding the photo.
And this second photograph is of my sister, Ella, enjoying herself in the rain after ballet. As you will notice, this picture is obviously different from my usual nature photography, and that is why I am quite interested in having critiqued.

Many thanks!

[old] holly---trees and keys

10/13/2011

4 Comments

 
Hi guys! I got a lot of mixed reviews (and a couple of weird looks) for some pictures I took. I pinned paintings to a tree, climbed it, and then took some photos. I like the way they turned out, and I'm thinking of doing similar photos  in the future. At the same time, though, I'd like to improve the photos.
The first pictures are pretty much finger paintings thumbtacked to a tree. I don't know what was going through my head when I thought of this.
Then I decided that the photos needed something. I wanted to add something small to the picture, so I took the keys out of my pocket and placed them between the branches.
Can I please get honest and/or constructive feedback? Thanks!
